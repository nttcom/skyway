    • General Questions

    Q. What is WebRTC?

  • WebRTC is a technology which enables realtime P2P communication between browsers.
  • As part of the HTML5 standard, WebRTC is currently being standardised by W3C and IETF.
  • Until now, to perform P2P communication it was necessary to either install a specific application or plugin.
  • P2P communication can enable direct video chat, screen sharing and file sharing functionality.
  • Using WebRTC it is possible to perform such P2P communication using only your web browser.
  • It is easy to communicate without having to rely on your communication partner having the correct application installed.
  • In addition, it is possible to enable cooperation with other existing services.
  • WebRTC is gaining attention for it's future application areas.

    • Q. What is SkyWay?

  • SkyWay is a platform directed towards application developers, enabling the easy utilisation of WebRTC technology.
  • What are some of the merits of using SkyWay?
  • When using WebRTC to biuld an application, additional knowledge is required beyond web application development technologies. For example, you also need knowledge about networks and servers.
  • This might include session control, bypassing NAT, development and construction of mediation servers.
  • SkyWay provides all these complicated background features and wraps them up in a simple API for implementation in your projects.
  • As a result Web developers can easily develop WebRTC applications without worrying about the backend details.
  • SkyWay was originally released in December 2013 as the first WebRTC platform in Japan.
  • We offer APIs and development environments for application developers to develop either for Web or smartphones.
  • We have over 2200 developers on board, with SkyWay being utilised in a variety of environments.
  • SkyWay is opensource. Our libraries, source code, and developer documentation is publically available on GitHub.

    • Q. What is HTML5 Lab?

  • We have been developing NTT Communication's "HTML5 Lab" since July 2013.
  • This is a project with the aim of advancing new ICT services using HTML5.
  • We also opened Japan's first HTML5 technology site, "HTML5experts.jp" in July 2013.
  • Through seminars and study sessions, we aim to provide enlightenment about HTML5 and support for web service developers.

    Technical Aspects

    Q. In what ways does WebRTC differ from existing P2P technologies?

  • Until now P2P has required the installation of a native application or plugin.
  • Using WebRTC it is possible to perform P2P communication using only the functionality of your browser.
  • Can confidently communicate without ensuring all parties have the relevant application or endpoint hardware.
  • It is also possible to link with other existing services easily.

    • Q. How are you ensuring that your P2P connections are secure?

  • P2P communication is always encrypted through SRTP and DTLS, so data transmission cannot be eavesdropped on by third parties.

    • Q. Which browsers are supported?

  • Chrome, Firefox and Opera are WebRTC ready.
  • IE does not have WebRTC support, but the new Edge browser will in future support WebRTC.
  • Safari remains unsupported, with future plans for support unknown.
  • See http://iswebrtcreadyyet.com/

    • Q. What communication delays are involved?

  • If the network condition is good, connections on the same network will be tens of ms, over the internet will be a few hundred of ms.
  • If network conditions are bad, delays can reach seconds.
  • In the case of crossing NATs through a STUN (intermediary) server, the delays can be larger.

    • Q. How good is the audio and video quality?

  • Audio uses a codec named "Opus", with bitrates in the range of 6~510Kbps.
  • The bitrate can change to suit the network conditions.
  • Bad network conditions are on the level of a mobile phone call, good network conditions have audio quality on par with MP3 files.
  • Recently TwitCasting has adopted Opus. They are aiming to attract musicians through the high audio quality.
    • http://japan.cnet.com/news/service/35069805/
    • http://japan.cnet.com/image/l/storage/35069805/storage/2015/09/02/d9e405eb3bdca786be43500936e7d37b/150902_t_01.jpg
  • Video uses the VP8 codec, with a bitrates in the range of 100~2000Kbps.

    • Q. What codecs are being used? What bitrates do they offer?

  • See above.

    • Q. What is VP8?

  • VP8 is a royalty-free video codec offered by Google.
  • Currently the H.264 standard is popular, due to it's hgih quality and compression rate.
  • H.264 incorporates patented technologies, and it's usage can therefore require license fee payments.
  • Google released the royalty-free VP8 codec for the browser for this reason.
    • http://gigazine.net/news/20140427-google-webm/

    Q. What is VP9?

  • VP9 is the successor to VP8 with higher picture quality.
  • With the same picture quality, the bitrate is halved in comparison to VP8.
  • It is being developed as a more efficient alternative to rival H.264 and H.265 codecs.
    • http://gigazine.net/news/20150407-youtube-vp9/

    Q. Is H.264 not supported?

  • H.264 has recently been adopted by the WebRTC standard, but is not yet supported by every browser.
  • Currently the H.264 standard is popular, due to it's hgih quality and compression rate.
  • H.264 incorporates patented technologies, and it's usage can therefore require license fee payments.
  • It was adopted by WebRTC after Cisco opensourced the standard last year.
  • But due to lack of support in all PC & mobile browsers, H.264 is not yet supported by SkyWay.
    • FireFox ：Supported. However it is necessary to to set H.264 as preferred in the application-side settings.
    • Chrome ：Expected to be supported by the end of the year.
    • IE/Safari ：Some plugins offer support.
    • iOS/Android ：Libraries offer support.
